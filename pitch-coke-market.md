<p><ul><li>Raw Coke</li><li>Calcined Coke</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pitch coke market is divided into two primary types: raw coke and calcined coke. Raw coke, produced directly from the carbonization of coal tar pitch, is primarily used in aluminum and steel production due to its high carbon content and conductivity. Calcined coke, on the other hand, is obtained by heating raw coke to remove volatile matter, enhancing its purity and electrical conductivity, making it essential in the manufacturing of electrodes for the aluminum industry and other high-performance applications.</p></p>
