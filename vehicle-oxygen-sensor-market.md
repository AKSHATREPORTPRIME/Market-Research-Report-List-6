<p><p>The Vehicle Oxygen Sensor market includes two primary types: Titanium Oxide and Zirconia. Titanium Oxide sensors use a titanium dioxide element to measure oxygen levels in exhaust gases, offering faster response times and durability in high-temperature environments. Zirconia sensors, the more common type, utilize a zirconium dioxide ceramic element to generate a voltage signal based on oxygen concentration, providing high accuracy and reliability. Both types are crucial for optimizing engine performance, reducing emissions, and ensuring compliance with environmental regulations.</p></p>
