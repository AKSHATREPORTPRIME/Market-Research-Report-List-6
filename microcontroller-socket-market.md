<p><p>The microcontroller socket market features various package types, each serving different applications. DIP (Dual In-line Package) provides easy handling and soldering for prototyping. BGA (Ball Grid Array) offers higher density and better thermal performance for compact designs. QFP (Quad Flat Package) facilitates high-speed connections with a flat design. SOP (Small Outline Package) and SOIC (Small Outline Integrated Circuit) are widely used for their space efficiency and surface mount capabilities, catering to diverse electronic device requirements. Each type optimizes functionality according to design needs.</p></p>
