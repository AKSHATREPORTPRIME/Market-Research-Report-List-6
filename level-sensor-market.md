<p><ul><li>Contact Level Sensors</li><li>Noncontact Level Sensors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The level sensor market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact level sensors. Contact level sensors directly touch the material being measured, providing accurate readings for liquids and solids, making them ideal for applications like water management and chemical storage. Non-contact level sensors utilize technologies such as ultrasonic, radar, or laser to measure levels without physical contact, suitable for environments where contamination or high temperatures may be an issue, often used in bulk storage and hazardous materials monitoring.</p></p>
