<p><p>The nutritive sweetener market comprises two main types: artificial sweeteners and natural sweeteners. Artificial sweeteners, such as aspartame and sucralose, are synthetic compounds designed to provide sweetness without calories, often used in diet and low-calorie products. In contrast, natural sweeteners, like honey, agave syrup, and stevia, are derived from plant sources and offer varying nutritional profiles. Both types cater to consumers seeking healthier alternatives to sugar, but they differ in origin, taste, and perceived health benefits.</p></p>
