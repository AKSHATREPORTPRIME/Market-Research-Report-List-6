<p><p>The LED substrate market comprises various types, each with unique properties. Sapphire substrates, known for their high thermal stability, are widely used in blue and white LEDs. Silicon carbide (SiC) substrates offer superior thermal conductivity and voltage resistance, making them ideal for high-power devices. Silicon (Si) substrates are cost-effective and commonly used for specific LED applications. Gallium nitride (GaN) substrates are favored for their efficiency in blue and ultraviolet LEDs. Gallium arsenide (GaAs) substrates are used for high-frequency and high-efficiency applications. Other substrates include materials like quartz and polymers, catering to niche market needs.</p></p>
