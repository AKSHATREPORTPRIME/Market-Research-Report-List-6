<p><p>Superalloys in the automotive market primarily consist of three types: iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based alloys. Iron-based superalloys offer good strength and wear resistance, making them suitable for components like gears and engine parts. Nickel-based superalloys are known for their exceptional high-temperature stability, often used in turbine engines and exhaust systems. Cobalt-based superalloys provide excellent corrosion resistance and are used in specialized applications, such as high-performance valves. Each type is engineered to meet specific performance requirements in automotive applications.</p></p>
