<p><p>Electric drive buses are categorized into three main types: Hybrid Electric Buses (HEBs) combine internal combustion engines with electric propulsion, using regenerative braking to recharge batteries. Plug-in Hybrid Electric Buses (PHEBs) operate similarly but can be externally charged, offering extended electric-only range. Battery Electric Buses (BEBs) rely solely on electric power, using large battery packs for zero-emission operation. Each type balances energy efficiency, emissions reduction, and operational flexibility, catering to diverse urban transit needs while advancing sustainable public transportation solutions.</p></p>
