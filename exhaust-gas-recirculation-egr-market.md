<p><p>Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) is an emissions control strategy used in internal combustion engines. It reduces nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions by recirculating a portion of the engine's exhaust back into the intake air. This process lowers combustion temperatures, minimizing the formation of NOx during fuel combustion. EGR is increasingly adopted in both diesel and gasoline engines to comply with stringent environmental regulations.</p><p>The EGR market is experiencing significant growth, driven by rising environmental concerns and regulatory standards aimed at reducing vehicle emissions. Automotive manufacturers are investing in advanced EGR technologies to enhance engine performance and efficiency while meeting emissions targets.
