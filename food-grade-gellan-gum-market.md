<p><p>The food-grade gellan gum market is divided into two main types: high acyl and low acyl gellan gum. High acyl gellan gum offers a softer, more gel-like texture, making it ideal for applications in desserts, dairy, and sauces. In contrast, low acyl gellan gum provides a firmer gel and is often preferred in applications requiring stability across varying temperatures, such as in confectionery and sauces. Both variants are widely used for their gelling, thickening, and stabilizing properties in food products.</p></p>
