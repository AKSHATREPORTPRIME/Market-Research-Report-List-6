<p><p>The vehicle suspension system market includes two main types: independent and non-independent suspension. Independent suspension allows each wheel to move independently, enhancing ride comfort, handling, and stability, making it ideal for passenger cars and high-performance vehicles. Non-independent suspension connects wheels on the same axle, offering durability and cost-effectiveness, commonly used in trucks and heavy-duty vehicles. Both systems cater to different vehicle needs, balancing performance, comfort, and cost, driving demand across automotive segments.</p></p>
