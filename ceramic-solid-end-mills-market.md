<p><p>Ceramic solid end mills are categorized based on their alloy compositions: iron-based, nickel-based, and cobalt-based. Iron-based end mills are known for their strength and wear resistance, making them suitable for cutting processes with lower temperatures. Nickel-based end mills offer enhanced corrosion resistance and thermal stability, ideal for high-performance applications. Cobalt-based end mills provide superior toughness and hardness, making them effective for machining tough materials. Each type caters to specific industrial needs, influencing their market demand and application areas.</p></p>
