<p><p>The inorganic flame retardant chemical market is primarily divided into two types: halogenated and non-halogenated. Non-halogenated flame retardants, such as magnesium hydroxide and aluminum hydroxide, are environmentally friendly and exhibit efficient flame-suppression properties without harmful emissions. In contrast, halogenated flame retardants, which often contain bromine or chlorine, are highly effective at lower concentrations but can release toxic gases upon combustion. The choice between these types depends on regulatory requirements, environmental concerns, and specific application needs.</p></p>
