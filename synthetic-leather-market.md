Key trends include the integration of eco-friendly practices in manufacturing processes and a surge in the adoption of synthetic leather in the automotive and fashion sectors. <p><p>The synthetic leather market comprises several types, each with distinct characteristics. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) offers durability and affordability, often used in handbags and upholstery. Normal PU (Polyurethane) is softer and more flexible, making it popular for clothing and accessories. Microfiber PU mimics genuine leather closely and provides superior breathability and texture. Ecological Function PU emphasizes sustainability, often made from recycled materials and designed to be environmentally friendly, appealing to eco-conscious consumers. Each type serves different applications and market needs.</p></p>
