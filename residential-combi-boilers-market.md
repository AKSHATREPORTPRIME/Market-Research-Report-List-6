<p><p>The residential combi boilers market is primarily categorized into non-condensing and condensing boilers. Non-condensing boilers operate at higher temperatures, effectively providing hot water and heating but are less energy-efficient, resulting in higher emissions. In contrast, condensing boilers utilize advanced technology to recycle exhaust gases, enhancing efficiency and significantly reducing environmental impact. This makes them more suitable for modern energy standards. The choice between the two often depends on factors like energy efficiency, installation costs, and home heating requirements.</p></p>
