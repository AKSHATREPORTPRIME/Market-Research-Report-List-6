<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) materials are categorized into two main types: two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D). 2D COFs consist of layered structures with extended planar sheets, offering high surface areas and enhanced conductivity, making them ideal for applications in gas storage and catalysis. In contrast, 3D COFs feature interconnected frameworks that provide higher stability and structural diversity, suitable for applications in sensing, drug delivery, and energy storage. Both types exhibit unique properties that drive their use in advanced materials science.</p></p>
