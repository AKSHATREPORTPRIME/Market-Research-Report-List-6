<p><p>The intra-uterine contraceptive devices (IUCD) market includes two main types: copper IUCDs and hormonal IUCDs. Copper IUCDs use copper to create a toxic environment for sperm, providing long-term contraception without hormones. In contrast, hormonal IUCDs release progestin, thickening cervical mucus and thinning the uterine lining to prevent pregnancy. Both options offer effective, reversible contraception with unique benefits, catering to diverse preferences and medical needs among users. The market continues to grow as awareness and accessibility increase.</p></p>
