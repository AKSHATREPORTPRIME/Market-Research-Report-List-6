<p><p>The ion-exchange membrane market is categorized into two main types: heterogeneous membranes and homogeneous membranes. Heterogeneous membranes consist of distinct phases, often providing enhanced ion selectivity and stability, suitable for applications like water treatment and energy conversion. In contrast, homogeneous membranes feature a uniform structure, allowing for consistent ion transport across the membrane, making them ideal for energy storage and separation processes. Both types play crucial roles in advancing technologies for water purification and fuel cells.</p></p>
